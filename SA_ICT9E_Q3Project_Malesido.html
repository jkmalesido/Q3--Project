Keeping You Safe: 5 Tips on Avoiding Fire
1  Keep a fire extinguisher Since fire requires fuel, heat, and oxygen, a fire extinguisher cools down the heat, suppresses the fuel, and removes oxygen to prevent the fire from burning. There are various types of fire extinguishers, namely Class A, B, C, D, and K. They are all classified based on the fires they extinguish.
2  Never leave a fire unattended Gas stoves or fireplaces are one of the main places where a fire can occur. Properly check if the fire has been put out to prevent its spread.
3  Install smoke alarms Smoke alarms or detectors alert us, and decreases our chances of becoming fire victims. It warns us in advance that there is a potential problem which gives us a little bit more time to escape.
4  Caution with candles Definitely, scented candles make your homes smell better. However, they also have the potential to cause fire and smoke damages. Hence, always put out your candles when leaving a room or before you sleep. Place them somewhere far from flammable materials like fabrics. Moreover, never place your candles on uneven surfaces to prevent them from tipping over.
5  Create a fire escape plan Knowing that fires are usually unpredictable, having an emergency fire action plan ensures everyone safely exiting the burning home or building. This must be practiced (through fire drills) at least twice a year.
